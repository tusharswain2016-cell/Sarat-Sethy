"Service to the people of Anandapur is not just my purpose — it is my identity and my commitment."

Sarat Kumar Sethy is a dedicated social worker and BJP activist from the Anandapur constituency (SC-22) in Keonjhar district, Odisha. Born on 17 April 1975 to Late Gopinath Sethy, he holds a Master's degree in History from Utkal University, Bhubaneswar.

Having served as a teacher at Jemamani Girls' High School, Mareigaon from 1997 to 2004, Sarat Kumar Sethy built deep connections with the community through education and grassroots social work. His spouse Parbati Sethy is also a teacher, and together they represent a family committed to public service.

Today he serves as Working President of State Co-operative PACS and Vice-President of the LAMPS Employees Association of India, while continuing his tireless work for farmers, cooperative workers, and marginalised people across the region.

Career & Service

The Journey So Far

From classroom to cooperative boardroom — three decades of dedicated grassroots service across Keonjhar district.

1993
Dramatic Secretary — Anchalika Mahavidyalaya, Hatadihi

First elected position, demonstrating early leadership in cultural and community activities at college level, Keonjhar.

1996
Students Union President — Bonth Anchalika Mahavidyalaya

Elected President of the Students Union, championing student welfare, academic development, and community engagement.

1997–2004
Teacher — Jemamani Girls' High School, Mareigaon

Served for seven years shaping hundreds of young students in Mareigaon, while simultaneously conducting blood donation and medical camps throughout the region.

2004–05
Director & Asst. Secretary — Mareigaon Seva Samabaya Samiti

Elected as Director in 2004 and promoted to Assistant Secretary in 2005, strengthening cooperative services and farmer support in the community.

2012–2014
Social Relief — Fire Victims, Salabani, Sadanga, Bancho

Led relief operations for fire-stricken families and participated actively in Municipality elections in Baripada and Karanjia in 2013.

2015
DCB & State Co-op Bank Directors Elected Under His Leadership

Under his direct leadership, two directors were elected to the District Co-operative Bank and State Co-operative Bank, Bhubaneswar — strengthening cooperative representation for Keonjhar.

2019
MLA Candidature — Anandapur (SC-22)

Put forward candidacy for the Anandapur assembly seat with significant local support rallied across all Gram Panchayats of the constituency.

2020–Now
Working President — State Co-operative PACS · VP, LAMPS

Serving as Working President of State Co-operative PACS and Vice-President of LAMPS Employees Association of India, championing farmers and cooperative workers across Odisha.

2023
300+ Workers Brought into BJP — Keonjhar, Panchugochhia, Bancho

100+ workers at Hadagarh & Panchugochhia and 200+ workers from Congress & BJD joined BJP at Bancho under his direct leadership — a landmark organisational achievement.

Present
BJP Aspirant, Anandapur SC-22 — Continuing People's Service

Continuing grassroots work through plantation programmes, the Mera Miti Mera Desh initiative, and leading anti-corruption drives in Mareigaon, Jambhira, and Panchugochhia G.P.s.

Our Vision

Building a Better Anandapur

Six pillars guiding every initiative and effort for the people of Anandapur constituency.

01🌾
Farmer Empowerment

Strengthening cooperative credit access, PACS reform, and direct market linkages for farmers across all villages of Anandapur block.

02📚
Education for All

Improving school infrastructure and ensuring SC community children have full access to scholarships and higher education pathways.

03🏥
Health & Welfare

Regular medical camps, blood donation drives, and accessible primary healthcare for every village in the constituency.

04⚖️
Anti-Corruption

Zero tolerance for corruption in Gram Panchayats. Already led successful drives in Mareigaon, Jambhira, and Panchugochhia G.P.s.

05🤝
Cooperative Reform

Reforming PACS and LAMPS institutions to genuinely serve farmers, tribal communities, and daily-wage workers across Keonjhar.

06🌳
Green Anandapur

Continuing plantation programmes across Soso, Mareigaon, and Habaleswar Gram Panchayats as part of the Mera Miti Mera Desh initiative.
